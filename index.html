Community Fellow Software Engineer (Full Stack) - Code for America

October 2020 - October 2022

Software developer for a web app service for our partner, the City of Fort Collins, that has made income-qualified programs more accessible to the residents of the city (and now ARPA funded)! I utilized JavaScript, HTML and Python (via Django framework) to create the frontend and backend of the website to ensure a clean, responsive UI and devOps (via GitHub, Docker & Azure) designed to help residents of Fort Collins who are in need. The web app currently uses Microsoft Azure and Postgres to handle the database for our system to log financial information for residents and utilizes algorithms and various API's like the USPS API to automate the sign-up and verification process and make it as fast, efficient and user friendly as possible. Google Analytics and Microsoft Azure captures important metrics such as application completion, time required to finish and more as well as pinpointing bottlenecks in the system to streamline for future revisions.

Teaching Facilitator - Coding it Forward

June 2022 - August 2022

During the summer of 2022 I was actually hired as a paid volunteer to help support Coding it Forward and Second Day, two civic tech entities, in teaching and supporting a mixture of 20 under grad and graduate students exploring career opportunities in civic tech! These civic tech fellows were working remotely all throughout the country helping several state governments like Kentucky, Missouri and California and even the CDC! I facilitated eight, one hour teaching sessions over the course of two months for these civic tech fellows where I discussed civic tech as a whole, gave advice traversing the political landscape, building professional government relationships as well as talking shop and supporting them with tech advice for their projects. It was a very affirming and inspiring two months where I learned that the younger generation are tired of the status quo and they want to change things, and I could not have been happier in helping them get to their goal.

Associate Student Director - San Jose State University

October 2016 - May 2017

As a student liaison to San Jose State's first ever, Paseo Prototyping Festival and Competition, designed to challenging SJSU students to make San Jose a better place to live, work and play - Andrew was tasked with overseeing student collaboration and filling up the competition ranks. From social media engagements (Instagram and Facebook) to tried and true, boots-on-the-ground marketing (tabling, fliers) and face-to-face presentations to +20 student orgs, Andrew was able to drum up +300 participants for the competition. Through his connections across the many disciplines of SJSU, Andrew was able to achieve a turnout that included artists, engineers, industrial designers, business majors and bio-chem majors. Moreover, through his influence, Andrew was even able to bring forth 10 "guest projects" from the student orgs (SJSU's Hyperloop Project, SJSU's Robotics Club) that showcased their most prized projects either pertaining to their curriculum or simply their interests. from the various student orgs at SJSU. These guest projects were to drive the fact that this competition was a celebration of all things SJSU, not just the participants. Furthermore, through his previous experience as a two-time winning rapid prototyping competition leader himself and former civic technology fellow, Andrew was in a unique environment to be able to tell his story to the participants and became the vision strategist for students, giving them tips on how to succeed in a civic tech based rapid prototyping competition. Finally, on the day of the competition, Andrew was given the task of overseeing and managing people and resources to ensure that the festival went off without a hitch, and he succeeded!

Technology and Civic Engagement Fellow - Microsoft

July 2015 - July 2016

In conjunction with San Jose politicians and the local government infrastructure, Andrew's role as a Civic Tech Fellow, was to create a safer, healthier, more accessible San Jose through technology. Andrew's time at Microsoft was a very unique experience - from explaining IoT and civic tech to elementary school students, to creating a Raspberry Pi login system for Microsoft's partner, Code for America, Andrew had taken the phrase "wearing multiple hats" to a new level. Andrew's project during his fellowship was the first-of-it's-kind competition titled: Spartan Hacks, a Civic Tech Challenge. Andrew became the Program Manager for this competition with one thing in mind: to challenge SJSU students to create technology to make San Jose a better place for her citizens. Andrew coordinated and lead a team of SJSU students, partnered with his Microsoft teammates and worked closely with employees of San Jose Mayor’s office to deliver this competition. Andrew worked on many aspects of the project to ensure its success, including outreach, budgeting, negotiation, planning and cultivating business relationships. From creating a website to boots-on-the-ground advertising and attracting 100+ participants to engaging San Jose’s CIO, Shireen Santosham, as a keynote speaker - Andrew’s capacity for outreach is exceptional. Because of Andrew’s Spartan Hacks project, San Jose became a new testing ground for civic tech projects which has inspired dozens of students to explore this new purpose of technology.

Quick Facts

Client: City of Fort Collins

URL: https://getfoco.fcgov.com/

GitHub: https://github.com/Get-Your/GetFoco

Category: Web App (SAAS)

Tech: Django Backend; JavaScript Frontend; GitHub; Azure Hosting; Docker Containers; Twilio SMS; Google Analytics;

Get FoCo is Fort Collins' first income-qualified programs web-app SAAS designed to help its residents in need!

This project was born out of the frustrations and anxieties that I lived through during 2020 - 2021, with the world reeling in from Covid, it was obvious that government services needed updating. Get FoCo was designed to treat its clients not as a statistic, but as people.
